71% of ANZ Executives Put Personalization First: Why Data & AI Will Drive 2025 Growth
Closing the Gap: 62% Want Personalization, But only 35% Get It

In today’s fast-evolving business environment, growth no longer hinges on product innovation alone — it’s increasingly driven by how well companies connect with their customers. For Australia and New Zealand (ANZ), the message is loud and clear: Personalization and data unification are the strategic levers for 2025.

Recent industry research shows that 71% of ANZ executives identify Personalized customer engagement as their top growth driver. Close behind, 61% cite data unification as a critical priority, while 60% are deploying AI to support smarter and faster business outcomes. These numbers highlight a region laser-focused on creating experiences that are tailored, data-driven, and future-ready.

The Rising Demand for Personalization

Customers have never been more vocal about what they expect from brands. According to the same research, 62% of consumers want personalised offers and experiences. Yet the reality is sobering: only 35% of them actually receive such tailored engagement. This stark gap signals both a challenge and an opportunity. On one hand, businesses that fail to deliver risk customer disengagement and churn. On the other hand, those that successfully close the gap stand to unlock deeper loyalty, higher retention, and stronger revenue growth.

Why Data Unification Matters

Personalisation cannot exist in a vacuum. To deliver truly relevant experiences, businesses must consolidate their customer data into a single source of truth. Without unification, data remains siloed across platforms, resulting in fragmented insights and disjointed customer interactions. It’s encouraging that 61% of ANZ executives are prioritizing data unification for 2025. By integrating data across touchpoints — from digital campaigns to customer service to transactional history — businesses can finally gain the holistic visibility required to act in real time.

AI as the Growth Accelerator

While personalisation and data unification provide the foundation, AI is becoming the accelerator. With 60% of ANZ leaders focusing on AI deployment, the region is set to harness predictive analytics, automation, and machine learning to deliver more timely and relevant engagement. Imagine a customer browsing online: AI can analyze patterns, predict intent, and serve up personalized recommendations in real time. Combined with unified data, this creates seamless experiences that feel intuitive and human — even though they are powered by advanced technology.

ANZ’s Advantage in APAC

Compared to the rest of the Asia-Pacific (APAC) region, ANZ is moving faster in recognizing the need for: 1.Data integration to eliminate silos. 2.Automation to streamline operations. 3. Real-time insights to deliver at the speed of customer expectation. This forward-thinking mindset positions ANZ businesses as leaders rather than followers. It also signals a competitive edge: those that invest now will shape the future of customer engagement across the region.

As 2025 approaches, the priorities are clear. Personalization at scale, powered by data unification and AI, will define winners and laggards in the ANZ business landscape. Consumers are ready — they’ve told us what they want. The only question is whether businesses are prepared to close the gap and deliver on the promise of truly personalised experiences.
